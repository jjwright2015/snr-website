League Rules

Saturday Night Racing Cup Series — Season 6 Rulebook

1-1: Driver Consent

By entering an SNR server it is assumed the entrant has read, understood, accepted, and agrees to abide by the rules in this document. These rules apply to any sanctioned League server, not just the Saturday race server. The SNR Discord is subject to the terms of this document as well.

1-2: Amendments

To ensure the best quality of racing, rules may be changed or amended between races. Notice will be given to drivers multiple days before the beginning of the next race or at the next drivers meeting.

1-3: Eligibility

Admins have the right to add/remove a driver based on knowledge of drivers' abilities.

1-4: Charters

As we have only 1 series in SNR this season, Charters have been put on the back burner until further notice. Previous Chartered drivers will have first choice on what number they would like to run.

2-1: Event Timeline

9:00 PM Practice Opens
10:00 PM 2-Lap Qualifying
10:05 PM Race Start
Post-Race Top 3 finishers required for broadcast interviews

All times Eastern Standard Time (EST) or Eastern Daylight Time (EDT).

2-2: Weather & Track State

Realistic weather enabled. Track condition starts at 15% rubber and carries over from practice to the race. Marbles will not be automatically cleared between sessions.

2-3: Setups & Fuel

Modified or Fixed setup — determined each week. 100% fuel load required.

2-4: Tire Limits

Tire limits will be implemented. The number of sets available will be announced prior to each race via the Discord Announcements channel.

2-5: Practice

Practice is for race prep, fuel runs, and setup work. Be courteous of fellow drivers and keep off the racing surface if possible. Horseplay or intentional wrecking during practice could result in pre-race penalties.

2-6: Qualifying

Qualifying scrutiny is set to strict. There will be 2 laps of qualifying at every track.

2-7: Starts & Restarts

All starts and restarts use the iRacing restart zone mechanic. The leader has lane choice on both starts and restarts and must declare at the one-to-go signal. No changing lanes before the start/finish line and no laying back — violations result in a pass-through penalty. Up to 3 GWC overtime attempts for all oval events.

2-8: Overtime

For all oval events there will be up to 3 attempts at a Green-White-Checkered finish.

2-9: Caution Flag

Drivers who fail to yield to a caution flag may be penalized post-race. This includes driving through wrecks at unsafe speed (ARCA braking), contact under yellow, or passing the pace car. Admins may throw a caution for extraordinary circumstances.

Road Courses: Full course cautions are disabled. Local cautions will be displayed instead. Caution rules still apply.

2-10: Black Flags

Drivers must comply with all iRacing-issued black flags unless the flag is believed to be unwarranted (e.g., going to pit road to avoid an accident, passing under yellow due to a slow car). If unsure, ask an Admin.

2-11: Superspeedways — Yellow Line Rule

No passing below the yellow line. "Below the yellow line" means any point of your left-side tires crossing the bottom of the lowest yellow line. Violation = drive-through penalty. Drivers forced below the line to avoid an accident will not be penalized. Drivers discovered post-race to have made an illegal pass below the line will be scored last on the lap completed.

2-12: Post-Race

Post-race is a cool-down lap. Horseplay or intentional wrecking will not be tolerated. Penalties include EOL at next event, probation, or suspension.

3-2: Caution Penalties

First 85% of Race -5 pts per caution caused
Last 15% of Race -10 pts per caution caused
  • 2+ cautions in a race = EOL penalty at next race
  • 3 cautions in a race = suspended for next scheduled race
  • 4 cautions in a race = suspended for the rest of the season

3-2a: Warning System

  • 2 warnings in 1 race = EOL at next race
  • 2 warnings in 3 races = 3-week probation
  • Warning while on probation = -5 pts + 1 week added to probation
  • 2 warnings while on probation = suspended 1 race

3-3: Probation

Drivers on probation are docked additional points for causing cautions:

  • Rookie drivers: -3 pts (first 85%) / -6 pts (last 15%)
  • All other drivers: -5 pts (first 85%) / -10 pts (last 15%)
  • 2 cautions while on probation = suspended 1 race

Placed on probation when: Caused a caution in consecutive races, 2 cautions in 1 race, caution in 3 of last 5 races, or at Admin discretion. New drivers are on probation for their first 3 race weeks.

Cleared by: Completing all probation races clean (clean practice, race, and post-race).

3-4: Connectivity Issues

Drivers whose connection is deemed too unstable to race will receive provisional (last place) points. Drivers will not receive laps back after reconnecting. In the event of a server crash or 5+ simultaneous disconnections, Admins will revert to the running order prior to the disconnection.

3-5: In-Race Chat

Keep chat to a minimum — race chat only (pitting intentions, track hazards). No open conversation or on-track disputes in chat.

3-6: Abuse

Threats of violence or abusive language will not be tolerated. Overt racist language results in immediate and permanent removal from SNR. Excessive arguing results in chat being disabled.

3-7: Intentional Wrecking

Intentional wrecking will not be tolerated. Penalties range from points deductions, probation, and suspension to permanent removal — determined case by case. Stopping on track is treated as intentionally trying to bring out a caution and will be reviewed post-race.

3-8: Teams

Teams: max 4 drivers, min 2. Team points are the average of all team drivers' race points. If fewer than 2 team drivers compete, no team points are awarded. Drivers must remain in their team's Discord voice channel during the race. Intentionally causing a caution for a teammate's benefit is a violation of Rule 3-7.

3-9: Pit Road

Stay to the right side of pit road. Drivers may not go through more than 4 pit stalls on entry or exit. Passing on pit road is permitted only if the car ahead is going significantly under the speed limit (5 mph or more). Any iRacing-issued pit road penalties will not be cleared.

3-10: Retaliation

Retaliation will not be tolerated. Penalties are at Admin discretion based on severity.

3-11: Slow, Damaged & Lapped Cars

Cars significantly off pace must move for lead-lap cars if not in an active battle for position. This applies to lapped cars, cars about to go a lap down, and heavily damaged cars. No driver may push another car back to pit road. All passes must be completed safely by both parties.

4-1: Regular Season Points

1st55 pts
2nd42 pts
3rd41 pts
4th40 pts
5th+39 pts, decreasing by 1 per position

No bonus points are awarded in Season 6.

4-3: The Chase (Playoffs)

  • Top 14 drivers advance to the Chase — determined by regular season standings
  • Chase begins Race 18 (Talladega)
  • Points reset: 1st = 2,025  |  2nd = 2,015  |  3rd = 2,012  |  4th = 2,010, then decreasing by 1 per position
  • Chase runs for 5 races through the end of the season

5-1: Driver Paint Schemes

SNR Drivers must follow iRacing and Trading Paints Terms of Service. Political paint schemes are generally permitted, but "anti" statements of any kind are not allowed. Broadcasters have additional restrictions. If unsure, ask an Admin before the race. Drivers using a prohibited paint may be removed from the server or required to change before the start of the race.

5-2: Custom Numbers

Numbers must be placed near the iRacing-standard locations: driver and passenger side doors (between wheel wells), passenger side front and rear bumper, and the roof (readable from the driver side). No other placement is permitted. Any readable font is allowed.

5-3: Discord Server

Drivers are required to be in the Saturday Night Racing Discord during all race events. This facilitates communication and allows broadcasters to pull drivers in for in-race interviews. Admins have the right to remove any member deemed detrimental to the league or its members.
